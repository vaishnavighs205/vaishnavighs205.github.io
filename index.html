PORTFOLIO — BIOMEDICAL ENGINEERING

Hamsini Gedela

I'm a biomedical engineering student at the University of Florida, and I've spent the last few years learning how to use computation to make sense of complex biological data and figuring out how that can actually help people. It started with an internship at eClinical Solutions [1], where I got my first real look at how messy clinical trial data gets standardized and made usable. That experience showed me how much of healthcare runs on good data infrastructure, and it made me want to go deeper.

Since then I've been working in the BEAT Cancer Lab [1], where I analyze genomics data from cancer patients and build automated statistical pipelines. Work that feels meaningful because it's directly connected to understanding how cancer behaves at a molecular level. At the same time, I'm at the Bolser Lab [1] writing simulation software and visualizations for computational neuroscience research, which has pushed me to think about how we model and interpret biological systems beyond just the data.

Through all of it, R, Python, Julia, SQL, and HPC workflows [1], what I've really been learning is how to build tools that make research faster, cleaner, and more reproducible. Where I want to take all of this is toward work that sits right at the edge of computation and clinical impact, whether that's in translational research, computational medicine, or health data science. I plan to get there by going deeper into biostatistics and machine learning for health data, and by staying close to research that actually has a shot at changing how patients are treated.
